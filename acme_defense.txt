Acme Defense Components is a 60-person manufacturing company based in Ohio. They produce specialty metal parts for two prime defense contractors. One of those primes recently told them that their next contract renewal, which comes up in eight months, will require CMMC Level 2 compliance. The company has no formal cybersecurity program, no documented security policies, and their IT is managed by one internal person who also handles the shop floor network. Their CEO, Mark, is aware of the requirement but has not made it a priority yet. Their CFO, Linda, is the one pushing internally because she understands the contract risk. They are currently using commercial Microsoft 365 and have no cloud security tools in place. Their estimated CUI footprint is small, roughly 15 users who touch contract data regularly.
